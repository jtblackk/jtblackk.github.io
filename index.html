Who I Am

I'm a user researcher, human-centered designer, and full-stack software developer from Charleston, South Carolina.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Clemson University in 2021. During this program I attended internships which gave me the expertise to design, develop, and evaluate software.

I wanted to explore more of the theory and practices used in user-centered design, so I joined Clemson University's Human Factors Psychology graduate program after attaining my Computer Science degree. Human Factors Psychology is concerned with the psychological theory underlying many of the design principles UX designers use.

In this program, I spearheaded numerous research projects on why users fall for phishing attacks and other types of online deception, and how interventions can be built to shield users from them. I also instructed numerous research design and statistical methods courses, lead research tearms, and have assisted in numerous collaborations that involved field interviews and other hands-on research.

With my experience, I have the skills to identify consumer needs; research, prototype, and evaluate potential solutions; then spearhead and evaluate the implementation of a functional system. Ultimately, I want to use my expertise in user-centered design to build technology that makes people
happier.
